Brand Identity Guideline

These guidelines describe the basic rules and usage of designing with or reproducing the Edible Arrangements brand identity. The brand goal is to make people smile using bright colors and playful illustration. That being said, please follow the branding guideline closely and avoid deviation to maintain the integrity of the brand.

Logos

Logo Usage

Sizing

The logo is spatially related based off of its x-height value. In print publications smaller than legal size (17" x 11"), this x value should not exceed 2 inches and should not be smaller than 1/10 of an inch.

The logo may be larger in case of use on exterior signs and billboards.

Spacing

Minimum spacing is relative to the size of the logo. A distance of 1/2x is applied as a part of the logo as padding. The logo may be enclosed in a white box under specific circumstances, or a red box as seen in the next example. With either of these cases, 1/2x in space is provided from the original logos surrounding square at each edge.

An example of the above statement with a red background.

When positioned around other logo elements, provide an additional 1/2x spacing from the edge of the box model with padding. This would make the total minimum distance from the edge of the logo 1x. The total logo space should take up 8 1/2x by 8 1/2x in space.

Orientation

Always face the logo upwards, and so that the text reads from left to right. Do not turn the logo horizontally or angle it. The logo must lie flat on a horizontal plane.

Coloring

Background Color:

Tangerine is always used as the background color behind the logo. Never use another color. White is acceptable as an alternative to Tangering. If color is used behind the logo in a square, it must be the specific shade of Tangerine outlined in this style guide.



General Color:

Do not alter the color of the text or fruit illustrations from the logos provided. They must match the original colors chosen for them.

Background

Edges:

When using a colored background, always have sharp edges without any rounded corners.

Shape:

Make sure the shape is a perfect square with the logo centered. Do not squash this shape or change its proportions.

Photography

Overlapping with Photo:

If demonstrating a scene, scene and other photography is permitted. The logo must be inclued with its orange background on this type of photography. Refer to the sizing and spacing requirements for placement.

Product Photography:

Products are always photographed on a white background to best showcase them to the consumer. Use the full color logo and refer to the sizing and spacing requirements for placement.

Additional Assets

Pattern

The pattern is composed of illustrations drawn from the Edible Arrangements logo. It is used in various print media and packaging, such as bags and boxes. The pattern can also be used to line paper and as a fun accent. If used in print, it should be the sole element on that page, and never used in combination with other elements.

Characters

Jeff the Bug:
Jeff the Bug is an explorer. To be particular, Jeff is a firefly. He flies onto fruit to guide children in their learning about fruit. He is a teacher and adventure junkie.





Mark the Worm:
Mark is a bookworm. He knows everything about fruit inside and out (get it, because he's a worm -- and he's been inside the fruit). Mark loves reading and researching, and he's a little bit of a goofy nerd who can answer any fruit question you have, right down to the sugar content of fiji apples.

Icons

There are several fruit icons associated with the brand. All fruit icons are at least two colors, and must match colors from the provided spectrum above. All icons consist of an offset base color (a "loose" and "flownig" aesthetic) and an outline with rounded edges. These illustrations are smooth and contain no harsh lines.
